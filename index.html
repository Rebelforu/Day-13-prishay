On the way you make people feel
You make people feel like the most interesting version of themselves
Not by complimenting them. By being genuinely curious about who they actually are — not the curated version they show everyone else. The unpolished, unedited, still-figuring-it-out version. You want that one. And the moment someone feels that from you, something in them relaxes and expands. You do this so naturally you probably have no idea how rare it is.
On how you love

On your laugh
The real one — the unguarded one — changes the room
Not the polite version. The real one: full, unplanned, completely uncalculated. When it comes out, it gives every person in the room permission to stop performing and actually be present. You have probably saved more difficult moments with that laugh than you will ever know. It is one of the most genuinely generous things about you.
4
On your honesty

When you say something, you mean all of it

No hedging. No strategic phrasing. When you tell someone they matter, they matter — in full. The weight your words carry is a direct result of never having been careless with them. People trust what you say in a way they do not always extend to others.

5
On your strength

You carry hard things without becoming hard

Every difficult season tried to calcify you — to make you smaller, more defended, less willing. You came through every single one still warm. Still open. That is not passive. It is one of the most deliberate and courageous choices a person can make, and you have made it every time, mostly without anyone seeing.

6
On your name

Prishay — rare in every possible way

It sounds like something poets would have invented if they had known you first. Rare. Specific. Unmistakable. It does not sound like anyone else because it is not. It was made for exactly one person, and it fits. The same way you do not quite fit any available category — because you were never designed to. You are your own kind entirely.

7
On your presence
Your absence is felt in rooms
Think about what that means — it means rooms notice when you are not there. Something is different, slightly less settled, without quite the same quality of ease. That is what it means to have presence: not that you demand it, but that you generate it simply by being. And people feel it before they can name it, every single time.

Chapter Three  ·  On this moment

On being in the middle of something

The middle of becoming something is one of the most disorienting places to exist — and one of the most important ones.

You are no longer the version of yourself from a few years ago. You have outgrown her so completely that looking back at certain decisions she made almost feels like reading about someone else — someone you understand and do not judge, but are clearly no longer. And yet you have not fully arrived at the person you are becoming. You are in the crossing. The between. And it is uncomfortable in a way that is genuinely hard to explain to someone who is not in it.

"The discomfort is not a sign that something is wrong. It is a sign that something is happening."

What I want you to understand — what I need you to actually hear — is that this exact place you are in right now is not the waiting room before your real life begins. This is your real life. The uncertain middle of it is not filler. It is where the most important things are being decided and built: your knowledge of who you are, your clarity about what you will not accept, your bone-level certainty about your own worth. Those do not come from the resolved chapters. They come from this.

And thirteen days from your birthday — walking through all of it, carrying the questions, staying warm through everything that tried to make you less warm — you are the most worth celebrating version yet. Not despite the in-between. Because of it.

Chapter Four

To you —
sweetly

A letter for the version of you that sometimes forgets how good she is. This one is specifically and entirely for her.

For Prishay  ·  April 29, 2026
Prishay —

You are so good at noticing the people around you. At seeing them — actually seeing them, not the version they perform, but the real one underneath. You notice when someone is tired before they say so. You notice when someone needs a conversation to go somewhere more honest and you gently, without announcement, take it there. You notice the small things: the particular way someone lights up talking about one specific thing, the detail they mentioned once that you remembered for months. You notice, and then you act on what you notice, and the people around you feel it in ways they will not always be able to articulate.

I want you to know that you deserve someone noticing you that way too. Not occasionally — consistently. Not when it is easy — especially when it is not. You deserve the same quality of attention you give, aimed back at you with the same warmth and the same care. You are not too much for that kind of love. You are exactly the right amount. Anyone who has made you feel otherwise was simply not paying the right kind of attention.

And I want to say something about April 29th — not as a birthday, but as a day that belongs to you. A day to receive. To let the good things come toward you without redirecting them, without explaining why they are not necessary, without checking first whether you have earned them. You have. You do. You always have. The version of you that exists right now — thirteen days out, still becoming, carrying everything — is the one this day is for. All of her. Not just the finished parts.

Walk into it like someone who knows they are loved. Because you are. Properly. Fully. Without a single condition.
